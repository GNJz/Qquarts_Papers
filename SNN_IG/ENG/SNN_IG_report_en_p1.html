Abstract

We examined whether a simple threshold‑gating mechanism—modeled as a proportional reduction of the spike threshold—amplifies collective responsiveness in a small spiking neural network (SNN). In a 5‑neuron LIF network receiving a pulse input from 0.1–0.7 s, we compared baseline (α=1.0) against IG on (α=0.7; 30% lower threshold). The total spike count was 0 at baseline versus 86 with IG on, indicating a qualitative transition from no response to network‑wide spiking under identical input. This constitutes a minimal reproducible example; broader validation is left for future work.

Interpretation

A 30% relaxation of the threshold is sufficient to tip the network from a non‑spiking to a collectively spiking regime. We interpret this transition as the combined effect of (i) individual neurons approaching threshold and (ii) positive feedback due to recurrent excitation, i.e., a collective critical phenomenon in this minimal network.
